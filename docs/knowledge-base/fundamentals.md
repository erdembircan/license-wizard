# Fundamentals

Software licensing is built on copyright law. When you write code, copyright protection applies automatically — but that does not mean others can use it. A license is what grants others permission to use, modify, and distribute your work under specific terms.

Understanding the legal foundation helps avoid common pitfalls: repos without LICENSE files, mismatched declarations, and placeholder text left in license files. These mistakes can create legal ambiguity and compliance issues.

This section covers the basics of copyright as it applies to source code, why explicit license files are necessary, and the most frequent errors developers make when licensing their projects.
